The Dollhouse

A life-size dollhouse replica, modeled after a turn of the century Victorian home, where participants were encouraged to discover haunted elements by interacting with objects that trigger light and sound.

Previous Next

Modeled after a turn of the century Victorian home, The Dollhouse was a piece comissioned for and featured at Burning Man 2019. The back side of the home had the illusion of being completely exposed to open playa, but was enclosed with clear acrylic paneling. This allowed citizens of Black Rock City to see the illuminated structure, and the participants within, from afar. There were two stories, each with two rooms, conventional to the Victorian era: ballroom, kitchen, bathroom, and bedroom. Art pieces in the home included photographs and paintings of a family long past, ghostly porcelain dolls, oddities, remnants of a seance gone wrong, an infinity mirror, and window etchings, all of which will reacted to human touch and/or presence. We hoped to create an ethereal world on playa, captivating and enticing people to play and discover more secrets within.



The house was designed for various levels of participation. Touching knobs, handles, and window etchings triggered sonic responses, but each room additionally had a unique interactive focal point. In the ballroom, In the ballroom, there was a seance table, painted as a ouija board. Participants could touch components of the table and watch as projection mapped animations revealed themselves. The kitchen featured a butcher block where participants could lay their hands, while a sensor took their pulse, and watch as a heart above would light up to their specific heartbeats. The bathroom had an infinity mirror, rigged with microphones to mimic and mutate any sound made in the room. In the bedroom, participants found a shelf of dolls that rotated their heads to follow their every movements.
